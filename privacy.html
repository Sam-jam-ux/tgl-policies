1. Introduction

The Good Life Path (TGL) is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect, how we use it, and your rights regarding our SMS/WhatsApp messaging program for community health screenings.

2. Information We Collect

  • Personal Information: Name, phone number, and optional email provided during opt-in.
  • Consent Records: Date, time, and method of opt-in (keyword, checkbox, or double opt-in reply).
  • Program Activity: Message delivery logs, opt-out requests, HELP requests, and health result categories (Low/Medium/High).
  • Technical Data: Device type, carrier, and other metadata automatically collected for compliance and troubleshooting.

3. How We Use Information

  • To deliver your health screening results via SMS/WhatsApp.
  • To maintain accurate opt-in/opt-out compliance logs.
  • To respond to HELP requests or customer support inquiries.
  • To comply with CTIA, carrier, and legal requirements.

4. Sharing of Information

No mobile information will be shared with third parties/affiliates for marketing or promotional purposes. Information may be shared with subcontractors or service providers strictly for support services (e.g., messaging platform, customer support). All categories exclude text messaging originator opt-in data and consent; this will never be shared with third parties.

5. Opt-Out & Consumer Control

You can stop receiving messages at any time by replying STOP. You will receive a confirmation that you have been unsubscribed. You may re-enroll later by repeating the opt-in process.

6. Data Retention

Consent logs and message history are retained for compliance purposes as long as required by law or carrier regulations, after which they are securely deleted.

7. Security

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect your data. No system is 100% secure, but we continually review and update our practices.

8. Children’s Privacy

This program is intended for adults. We do not knowingly collect information from minors without parental/guardian consent.

9. State & International Privacy Rights

  • California Residents: You may request details about the information we hold and request deletion under the CCPA.
  • Other U.S. States: If your state has enacted consumer privacy laws, you may have similar rights to access or delete personal information.
